August Reading List

Ilona Andrews, Chloe Neill, Faith Hunter and Mark Lawrence
      In August, I wasn't feeling very author-y or writer-like and read more than wrote :( As was mentioned in the previous reading list, I finished Ilona Andrews' Magic Rises, the 6th book in the Kate Daniels series, first and I have nothing great to report about it. The team heads over to the Europe and I disliked nearly everything in the story from that point on. Curran, our protagonist's lover has changed completely and us readers have the read about our fearless kicking butt, taking names heroine almost navel gazing... What did I do wrong? Oh noes! Why does he hate me? The given reason doesn't sell it for me. If what is written is true (I know, it's fantasy fiction with magic and weres and 'true' is up for debate), then Curran is no longer the strong lion man. He's a weak kitten. Disappointing.

      I had plans to read Kate Daniels' 7th book, but after the 6th I waited and devoured The Veil by Chloe Neill. The Veil is an urban fantasy, first in the series Devil's Isle. It's a great idea! The veil is torn and war hits the us, centered around New Orleans. It was torn by supernatural creatures, fairies and the like and they brought magic in with them. I wish I loved the writing... It wasn't bad, but the romance partners didn't have much charisma. It, the plot, also just didn't feel possible. I know people like to stay no matter the natural disaster, Chernobyl for example, but I had a hard time believing that there was so little federal presence 7 years after a war on US soil happened. I'll definitely consider reading the 2nd book in the series because I do like the world building done, maybe I just missed things while reading that would have clicked things for me.

      Then I read book 7 of the Kate Daniels series, Magic Breaks. It was better than the previous, thankfully. Most noticeable, Curran was gone for a bunch of it and, while that annoyed me, it was good he was. It annoyed me for two reasons. One, because I hadn't expected he would leave after the horrible way he acted in the previous novel. Two, I had to read her pining for mister perfect awesome wonderful, can do no wrong Curran. Ugh. However, to the good, Ilona Andrews did a good job on the passages that focused on despair. I thought it was very strong writing. Or maybe it's just easy to connect me to depression right now?ANYWAY, I liked the ending of the novel and it seemed a series ending almost. There are 3 more books in the series, but I”m happy to stop now on a good note. I will likely go back to finish the Kate Daniels series but I'm going to take a break from it for now. Yes, it's worth it to read the series but maybe skip book 6 :)

      Changing tactics, I picked up an old series that I appeared in my monthly reading previously, Soulwood. Specifically, book 5, Spells For The Dead by Faith Hunter. I had hoped for the main character to change in the next novel, to get maybe more violent or follow more baser instincts, but it didn't happen. This book seems to have skipped over some of the building as we mostly just keep running across dead bodies. Seems like the PsyLED team is really bad at the their jobs. Sure, they stick people in null rooms eventually, but I'm not a fan of so many needless deaths just to continue a plot point. Also, if you preferred the supporting characters over the main, like me, you are potentially in for some disappointment. If you liked the empath Tandy or the cat shifter Rick, you may not like this book as they are barely included. In my case, I want to read a book from the point of view of T. Laine, the witch. She was heavily included in this novel and I now have much more of an urge to know what she's thinking over Nell!

      Lastly, I decided I needed more gritty stuff and picked up Red Sister, the 1st novel in the Book of the Ancestor series, by Mark Lawrence. He writes masterfully and this novel started no differently! I read it so fast, I had to stop myself and re-read sections as I was afraid I would miss things. Mark Lawrence is a wizard at world building and I felt I was part of Nona's world early on. My warning to you readers is that this novel is a 'coming of age' novel. Classic one where the main character, Nona, gets more powerful as she trains, learns and makes mistakes. So if you like those types of novels, you can't go wrong picking up this book! If you don't care for that style, you may not like this novel, but the writing is so good, I suggest you try it anyway. Around the halfway point in the novel, I ran across my first disappointment and it got worse rather than better. Nona seems to have such terrible self-esteem or self-worth that she allows people to beat her bloody without a single complaint. I didn't like reading that all. I also did not like the epilogue. It skips too many things and forces us readers to just accept things that changed/happened with characters without giving a hint of a reason why they changed or what happened. That left a poor taste in my mouth but the book was still so well written I will likely grab book 2!
